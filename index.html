Amsterdam Lisp & Scheme meetup

About

Amsterdam Lisp & Scheme meetup is a small community of lisp enthusiasts. Whether you are lisp expert or just curious, please join one of our future meetups, everybody is welcome!
We're constantly looking for new speakers. If you want to give a talk on any topic that has relation to lisp - from theory or history to practical applications that are built with lisp or use lisp in any way, please consider giving a talk and sharing your experience with others. Our meetup is not tied to any specific lisp language. Whether you use Common Lisp, Racket, Scheme or Clojure or any other language is equally interesting.

Stu; understanding “code is data is code”
01.05.2017, Michael Austin
Presentation video
The above mantra is fundamental to the lisp philosophy, but what does it mean in more tangible terms? I will describe the beginnings of stu, a lisp dialect made from first principles in order to explore this concept.
Red language overview
01.05.2017, Maxim Velesyuk
Presentation video
Red looks to the “code is data” idea from a different angle, in combination with modern techniques and high quality engineering makes it very powerful and practical language. We’ll take a look at Red in this talk.
